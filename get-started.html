Start Your Sheepdog Trial Journey

From watching your first trial to training your own dog — here is the clear, practical pathway into the world of sheepdog handling.

This Month This Year Long Term

This Month — Observe & Learn

Short Term

Begin by attending local sheepdog trials. Watching in person builds understanding faster than any video. Observe:

  • How handlers position themselves
  • When they intervene vs when they trust the dog
  • How sheep react to pressure, distance, and terrain
  • Strategies shift depending on wind, slope, and flock behaviour

You can find regional trials and calendars on the International Sheepdog Society website:

https://isds.org.uk

This Year — Begin Your Training

Medium Term

If you own a suitable dog (Border Collie or herding breed), begin with professional mentoring. Experienced handlers prevent training mistakes that are difficult to fix later.

If you do not own sheep, explore:

  • Renting a small field (like Victoria Graham)
  • Joining shepherding associations with shared training spaces
  • Volunteering on farms in exchange for training access
  • Attending structured classes

Document your progress if you enjoy social media — honesty and real improvements inspire others.

Pro Tip: Start with basic obedience before introducing sheep work.

Long Long-Term — Path to championship

Long Term

With years of steady progress, you can set your sights on competition. The World Sheepdog Trials (held every 3 years) gather elite handlers worldwide.

Study championship footage online, observe top handlers' timing, pressure control, and calmness.

Famous examples from your transcript:

  • Julie Hill — International Supreme Champion
  • Chelsea Elkin — Young Handler Champion at age 14
  • Victoria Graham — Progressed from beginner to English National competitor

Your journey mirrors theirs — patience, dedication, and consistency.
